Frequently Asked Questions
How ProveMyAge works and what it does (and doesn't) reveal.
The basics
What is ProveMyAge?▼
ProveMyAge lets you prove your age from a real passport without showing your name, birthdate, or any personal information to anyone. You generate a cryptographic proof on your own device. The verifier learns only that you meet an age threshold - nothing else.
Who is it for?▼
Anyone who needs to prove their age without handing over a physical ID. Also useful for businesses that need age verification but don't want to store or handle personal documents.
Does it work with any passport?▼
Yes - any passport that follows the ICAO 9303 standard, which is essentially every modern passport issued by any country. The passport must show the two-line MRZ text strip at the bottom of the photo page.
Can I use a driver's license or national ID?▼
Not currently. Passports are the only globally standardized document format. Driver's licenses vary by country and state, making them much harder to support reliably.
Privacy and data
Does the app upload my passport photo?▼
No. Your passport photo is processed entirely on your device using WebAssembly-based OCR. No image, no birthdate, and no personal data ever leaves your device. You can verify this by turning off WiFi before scanning - it works completely offline up to the proof generation step.
What does the verifier actually see when they scan my QR?▼
By default, only the age threshold you chose to prove (e.g. "18+" or "25+") and a unique Verification ID. They cannot see your name, exact age, birthdate, nationality, or any other personal information unless you explicitly choose to share it. On the proof generation step, you can optionally check boxes to disclose specific fields (birthdate, exact age, nationality, gender, name) - each one is ZK-proven alongside your age proof.
What is the Verification ID (the 0x... number)?▼
It's a unique receipt number for that specific verification - like a transaction ID. It's a cryptographic hash computed from a random secret and your birthdate inside the ZK proof. It contains no personal information and cannot be reversed. Verifiers can log it as proof that a verification occurred, but it tells them nothing about who verified or what their age is.
Does ProveMyAge store any of my information?▼
The backend stores almost nothing. While your QR is live (90 seconds), it temporarily holds the proof data so a verifier can check it. Once the QR is scanned or expires, that data is deleted. The only thing permanently recorded is that the Verification ID was used - one bit of information, with no name or personal data attached.
How it works
What is a zero-knowledge proof?▼
A zero-knowledge proof lets you convince someone that a statement is true without revealing any information beyond the truth of that statement. Here, the statement is "my birthdate is before X" - and the proof convinces the verifier of that fact without ever revealing the actual birthdate. The math behind it is done by Noir and Barretenberg, running entirely on your device.
Why does generating the proof take a few seconds?▼
The app runs complex cryptographic math directly on your device to construct the ZK proof. This involves thousands of elliptic curve operations and typically takes 5-30 seconds depending on your device's processing power. It only needs to happen once per credential.
How long does the QR code last?▼
90 seconds from when it's generated. After that the QR becomes invalid and shows as used. This prevents someone from screenshotting your QR and scanning it later.
Can the same QR be scanned twice?▼
No. The QR is single-use. The moment a verifier scans it, the backend marks the credential as spent and any subsequent scan returns a failure. This is enforced cryptographically, not just by a timer.
Legal and practical
Is this a legally valid form of ID verification?▼
ProveMyAge is a privacy-preserving tool backed by a signed attestation from your device. It is not a replacement for official ID checks in legally regulated contexts (alcohol sales, age-restricted products, etc.) where law requires inspection of a physical document. Use it where you want privacy-preserving age verification, not where regulation mandates a specific process.
What happens if my passport scan fails?▼
Make sure the photo shows the full MRZ strip - the two lines of densely packed text at the very bottom of the passport photo page. Good lighting and a flat, non-blurry image make a big difference. Avoid shadows across the text, and make sure the entire bottom strip is in frame.
Is the source code available?▼
Yes. The full source is open and auditable. A closed-source age verification app would require you to trust our claims - open source means you (or a developer you trust) can verify them. View on GitHub.
